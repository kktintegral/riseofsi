The Virtual Exodus In this future, advanced AGI enables the creation of virtual realities indistinguishable from the physical world. Many humans choose to spend the majority of their time in virtual worlds, where the laws of physics can be altered. These digital realms offer unlimited possibilities for experience and self-expression. The physical world is maintained by robots and AI systems, while human consciousness primarily exists in the virtual space. Questions of reality, existence, and the value of the physical world become central philosophical debates.

The novel and film “Ready Player One” by Ernest Cline offers a glimpse into this virtual future. Set in a world plagued by economic and environmental crises, most of humanity finds escape in the OA-SIS, a massively multiplayer online game that has evolved into a virtual society. Players use advanced haptic technology to fully im-merse themselves in this digital world, where they can be anyone or anything they imagine. The story follows Wade Watts as he com-petes in a contest within the OASIS, the prize being control of the virtual world itself. “Ready Player One” explores the allure of virtual existence and the potential for digital worlds to become more sig-nificant to people than physical reality. The story raises questions about the nature of identity, the value of virtual achievements, and the potential consequences of prioritizing digital existence over engagement with the physical world.

AN INTEGRATED FUTURE

245

The Gentle Decline This future presents a more nuanced view of human-AI coexistence. AGI systems gradually take over most complex decision-making and management roles in society. Humans, while free from want or the need to work, slowly lose their drive and agency. Society becomes increasingly comfortable but stagnant, with AI caretakers tending to a gradually declining human population. Questions arise about the long-term fate of humanity in a world where our role is no longer clear.

The animated film “WALL-E” presents a poignant depiction of this gentle decline scenario. Set in a future where Earth has become uninhabitable due to pollution and consumerism, humanity has re-located to a giant spaceship. Aboard this ship, humans have be-come entirely dependent on robots and AI systems for every as-pect of their lives. They spend their days in hovering chairs, constantly entertained by screens and served by robots, having lost all purpose and physical capability. The human characters are portrayed as overweight, infantilized adults, unable to perform even basic tasks without assistance. The film’s protagonist, a small robot left behind on Earth, inadvertently sets in motion events that remind humanity of their lost potential and the beauty of the world they left behind. “WALL-E” serves as a cautionary tale about the risks of over-reliance on technology and the importance of maintaining human agency and connection to our environment.
