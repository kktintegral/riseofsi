At the most fundamental level, the potential of Superintelligence is shaped by the immutable laws of computation. The theory of computational complexity reveals intrinsic boundaries to what can be efficiently computed, even by the most advanced intelligence. NP-hard problems resist efficient solutions regardless of computational power, while the halting problem demonstrates that certain questions must remain unanswerable in finite time. These computational limits are not mere practical constraints, but fundamental truths about the nature of information and problem-solving. They suggest that even a Superintelligent entity would face insurmountable challenges in solving certain classes of problems or making particular types of predictions, irrespective of its processing capabilities.

Building upon this computational foundation, the laws of physics further define the landscape within which Superintelligence can operate. The expansion of resources available to AI is governed by the three-dimensional nature of space and the speed of light. This combination limits the rate at which an AI can spread to acquire more material, energy, and computational resources to O(tÂ³). The speed of light not only caps the pace of this expansion but also sets an absolute limit on information transfer and causal influence across the cosmos. Additionally, emerging theories such as quantum gravity and the holographic principle suggest that information storage and computation within a region of space scale with its surface area, not with its volume as we might expect. The laws of thermodynamics dictate the inevitable increase of entropy, while quantum mechanics introduces inherent uncertainties at small scales. These physical laws, intertwined with computational limits, form the stage upon which the drama of intelligence and Freedom unfolds.
