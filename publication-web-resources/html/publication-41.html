The transition from subhuman to superhuman intelligence will be rapid, as AGI is inherently scalable. Superintelligence is not a black swan event (à la Nassim Taleb), but it may be perceived as one for those unprepared. Some critics argue that discussions about AGI are premature and speculative, as true AGI may be far from being realized, if at all.

We will build superintelligence and direct it to all aspects of hu-man and environmental flourishing. However, a significant chal-lenge is the potential for AGI to exacerbate existing inequalities and power imbalances, both within and between nations. The devel-opment of AGI may also create new opportunities for human flour-ishing and fulfillment. By freeing humans from many of the repeti-tive and mundane tasks that currently occupy much of our time and energy, AGI could create space for greater creativity and personal growth. It could also enable new forms of collaboration and social organization, as individuals and communities are able to pursue their passions and interests with greater freedom and flexibility.

To address this challenge and opportunity, the development of AGI must be guided by a strong commitment to value alignment—ensuring these systems are designed and trained in ways that are consistent with human values and interests. We must ask ourselves: What common vision can bring humanity together to steer AGI’s influence as a unifying, not a destructive force? What ethical frame-work, what idealized aspiration, can harmonize the actions of ~10 billion humans and the superintelligent AGI systems we create to channel our combined efforts down a constructive path?

The first step then is to agree on a shared vision for the future. This vision will be the central foundation for a framework within which diverse stakeholders—governments, corporations, civil society, and individuals—can align their strategies and actions. This vision will be embedded within AGI itself as its guiding aspiration, ensuring that its actions align with our collective aspirations and ethical standards.

Can we articulate a positive vision for the future?
