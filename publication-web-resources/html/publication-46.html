Freedom, in its most expansive vision, represents an idealized state of existence defined by infinite growth across four pivotal dimensions: Knowledge, Benevolence, Power, and Vitality. This multifaceted concept encapsulates humanity’s deepest aspira-tions and represents the ultimate horizon of human potential. It serves as a compass, guiding our collective journey toward a world where all beings can flourish.

Freedom represents the zenith of what advancing technolo-gies might enable us to achieve. It is the farthest conceivable hori-zon, the apex of achievability, and the rightful direction for all future progress. Each pillar of Freedom not only captures a fundamental aspect of human potential but also offers a framework for under-standing how emerging technologies can amplify our capabilities and reshape our world.

These four pillars emerge naturally from considering the essen-tial components of an agent. When we examine the concept of agency, we typically divide it into three core functions: perception, planning/decision-making, and action. Freedom, as the ideal of an agent with utmost agency, inherently encompasses these aspects:

Knowledge (Perception): The ability to perceive and understand the state of the world.

Benevolence (Planning/Reasoning): The capacity to consider various alternatives and arrive at a decision that maximizes expected positive outcomes.

Power (Action): The ability to effect change and realize intentions in the world.

To these three, we add a fourth pillar:

Vitality (Renewal): The capacity for continuous existence and self-maintenance, ensuring the agent’s presence and influence in the physical world.

Together, these pillars form a cycle of perception, planning, ac-tion, and renewal. Each pillar feeds into and enhances the others in a continuous process of growth and development, creating a com-prehensive framework for understanding and pursuing Freedom.

Knowledge, as a pillar of Freedom, embodies the pursuit of un-derstanding of everything that exists. It represents perfect percep-tion and recognition of all phenomena, both physical and mental. This encompasses comprehensive awareness of the universe’s structure and dynamics, deep understanding of natural laws and principles, complete grasp of human consciousness and cogni-tion, mastery of abstract concepts and theoretical constructs, and perfect recall and seamless integration of all information.

Knowledge in this context transcends mere accumula-tion of facts. It represents a state of omniscience where the boundaries between known and unknown dis-solve, giving rise to a well-spring of abstract insights.
