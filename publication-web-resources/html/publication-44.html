In this context, we propose an ideal which we may call “Freedom” as a candidate for such a vision. Freedom here transcends the conventional definition of the word, which is the absence of external restraints, to represent an idealized state of achievement.

At the point of Freedom, the very fabric of time seems to dissolve, allowing everything to be entropically reversible. It’s a nexus of complete knowledge, absolute goodness, infinite power, and everlasting vitality.

At first, this might seem like an abstract, unachievable concept. Yet, it is not the attainment but the journey towards Freedom that matters. In mathematics, the concept of infinity is often used as a tool to better understand finite numbers. Freedom is a direction, not a destination—an infinite horizon guiding humanity’s progress.

Freedom finds resonance with the Free energy principle—a fundamental concept in physics and computational neuroscience. The Free energy principle states that intelligent systems, including living organisms, seek to minimize the discrepancy between their internal models and the external environment. In essence, they strive to maximize their freedom to act under uncertainty.

Freedom is intricately linked to the amount of controllable variation that can be exhibited in a system. It is about the capacity to explore diverse possibilities, to adapt, and to evolve. The greater the Freedom within a system, the greater its potential for both creativity and resilience.

In the pursuit of Freedom, one aligns personal preferences and with collective well-being. The attainment of Freedom for any individual is identical with the whole. Thus the pursuit of one individual is consistent with and requires the support of that of another and of the whole. In essence, the maximization of Freedom becomes a recursive definition of morality itself.
