The rapid evolution of artificial intelligence necessitates extraor-dinary investments into its underlying computational infrastruc-ture. The trillions of dollars flowing into this sector represent not mere extravagance, but a prescient strategic commitment with wide-ranging implications. The burgeoning market capitalization of tech giants like Microsoft, Google, NVIDIA, TSMC, Meta, Apple and Amazon—fueled in part by their AI initiatives—stands as an ear-ly testament to this investment’s potential.

While many researchers focus on the critical pursuit of greater computational and data efficiency in AI models, the need for robust infrastructure will remain paramount. Even as models streamline, anticipated real-world deployments will ensure a growing demand for powerful computational resources. This will unlock the potential for transformative AI applications across industries. We will integrate foundational models into the real-world, propelling change on an unprecedented scale.

Governments and corporations are investing trillions into an AI arms race rapidly permeating the global economy. Over the next few years, AI capabilities across both narrow and general domains could advance more than throughout history. Self-improving algorithms promise exponential capability growth, radically outpacing updating of regulations and oversight.

The advent of AGI heralds the emergence of a new form of intelligence on Earth—an intelligence born not of biology and millions of years of evolution, but of mathematics, code, and engineering breakthroughs over just the past few decades. AGI would mean the creation of adaptable, self-improving, general-purpose minds that can absorb information, make inferences, formulate strategies, and act with the flexibility characteristic of human cognition. AGI systems would exhibit key traits like common sense reasoning, abstract thinking, imagination, and open-ended learning.
