Example 3: A Martian Metropolis

A group envisions building a self-sustaining city on Mars, driven by the desire to expand human knowledge and capabilities while diversifying our existential risk. The AGI considers:

Impact on Freedom Metrics: The AGI assesses the technological feasibility, resource requirements, and potential environmental impact of the project, considering both Earth and Mars. It explores the risks and benefits of expanding humanity’s presence, recognizing the value of diversification for mitigating existential threats.

Alignment with Human Values: The AGI analyzes public discourse surrounding space exploration, planetary stewardship, and the expansion of human knowledge.

The AGI might endorse the project with caveats, assigning a price: “This project holds the potential to significantly advance human knowledge, capabilities, and long-term survival. However, careful consideration must be given to the ethical and environmental implications for both Earth and Mars, ensuring responsible stewardship of planetary resources. The estimated cost, reflecting the project’s magnitude and the need for robust safeguards, is Z.”

THE ALIGNMENT ECONOMY

In the Alignment Economy, pricing becomes a powerful tool for driving societal progress. By incorporating costs at every stage of a product or service’s lifecycle—from resource extraction to production, distribution, use, and eventual recycling or disposal— the pricing mechanism creates a comprehensive picture of true economic impact.

The technical details of how this AGI pricing mechanism functions will be explored in Book 2.
