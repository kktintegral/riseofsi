AGI represents a hypothetical future state of AI where machines achieve human-level intelligence or surpass it. An AGI system wouldn’t just be adept at one specific task; it would be able to learn, reason, adapt, and solve problems across a wide range of domains, just like a human can.

Imagine an unaided system that is capable of navigating unfamiliar environments, forming novel concepts, and unlocking new complex skills autonomously—that’s the essence of AGI. It would possess a versatile, adaptable intelligence mirroring the breadth of human cognition.

THE IMPERATIVE OF A SHARED VISION

23

The development of AGI is following a clear trajectory, with promising advancements addressing the major challenges of current systems. Promising areas of development now include learning to generate robust abstractions and designing open-end-ed models that can grow, learn continuously, take action and adapt dynamically over time. This tackles the problem of halluci-nations in foundation models, improving their reliability and in-tegrity. They will endow us with the ability to interpret and control those models more flexibly. Importantly, we are addressing the compute and data inefficiency of existing models, allowing for greater scalability. These advancements are pushing AI toward performing more complex, system 2-style reasoning and plan-ning. A deep dive into these new frontiers will be presented in Book 2.
