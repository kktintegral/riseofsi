The Cultural Revolution in China, launched by Mao Zedong in 1966, aimed to purge society of tradi-tional and capitalist elements and create a new socialist culture. However, the movement quickly descended into chaos and vio-lence as radicalized youth, known as Red Guards, attacked intellectu-als, artists, and perceived enemies of the revolution. This resulted in widespread social and economic disruption and a lasting legacy of trauma and division. The Cultural Revolution was driven by a politi-cal power struggle, a desire for ideological purity, social upheav-al, and economic disruption.
