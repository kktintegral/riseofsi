The revitalized laws, while aiming for universal applicability, must also navigate the complex terrain of collective and individual differences in a post-AGI world. A hierarchical ethical framework, encompassing universal, group, and individual layers, provides a structured approach to addressing these complexities:

The Universal Layer: This foundational layer establishes global-ly recognized ethical principles applicable to all AGI develop-ment and deployment. These principles, rooted in a shared understanding of human well-being and dignity, prioritize safety, security, and the prevention of harm. This layer ensures that AGI, regardless of its specific context or application, ad-heres to fundamental ethical guidelines.

The Group Layer: Recognizing that values, norms, and legal frameworks can vary significantly across different groups— whether they are nations, communities, companies, or other collectives—this layer allows for AGI to adapt and respond accordingly. For example, AGI interacting within a rural com-munity might require different considerations than when en-gaged with a major urban hospital. Similarly, a company might have specific ethical guidelines that AGI must take into ac-count. This layer ensures flexibility and responsiveness to the unique needs and values of diverse groups while remaining grounded in the universal principles of the foundational layer.

The Individual Layer: This layer focuses on AGI personalizing its interactions to meet individual preferences and needs, respect-ing user autonomy and maximizing positive experiences. This personalization could involve tailoring an AGI assistant’s com-munication style or its approach to problem-solving to align with an individual’s personality and desires.

By integrating these three layers—the universal, the group, and the individual—the ethical framework for AGI becomes both globally consistent and locally adaptable. This approach ensures that AGI serves humanity in a way that is both ethically sound and sensitive to the rich diversity of human differences.

My personal experiences living in diverse countries, including Japan, the US, and Lebanon, among others, have deeply impressed upon me the importance of understanding and respecting cultural and individual differences. It is essential that we approach AGI development with this awareness, ensuring this technology is designed and deployed in a way that honors the full spectrum of human values and experiences.
