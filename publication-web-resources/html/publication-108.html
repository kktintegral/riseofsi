The Alignment Economy, with its focus on harmonizing prices with our collective pursuit of Freedom, compels us to rethink the very foundations of economic distribution. In a world where AGI-driven abundance has the potential to liberate us from scarcity, the traditional paradigm of work-for-income becomes increasingly inadequate. We require a system that not only provides for basic needs but also empowers individuals to actively participate in shaping this new world, while aligning their actions with both personal fulfillment and collective flourishing.

This is where the concept of Basic Income enters the picture, but perhaps not in the way we traditionally conceive it. While providing every individual with a guaranteed income floor has undeniable merits, the term itself may carry cultural baggage, evoking notions of dependency or unearned rewards. The Alignment Economy offers a more foundational perspective, suggesting a framework where economic empowerment is deeply intertwined with the very fabric of Freedom.

Imagine a system where the act of being alive has inherent value. This might seem counterintuitive in a traditional economic framework, but in the Alignment Economy, where prices reflect alignment with Freedom, even seemingly passive activities can contribute to collective well-being.

Consider the simple act of sleep. From a purely economic perspective, sleep might be viewed as unproductive time. However, through the lens of Freedom, sleep becomes an investment in Vitality, enhancing an individual’s capacity for creative and meaningful action. Superintelligence, recognizing this connection, might assign a negative price to sleep, up to a point, effectively rewarding individuals for taking care of their well-being.

This logic extends beyond sleep to encompass the very essence of being alive. Nobel laureate economist Amartya Sen, in his “Capabilities Approach,” argues that true development lies in

expanding people’s capabilities—their freedom to achieve the things they value. This includes not just economic resources, but also access to education, healthcare, and political participation. The Alignment Economy, embracing this expansive understand-ing of human value, ventures beyond a simple basic income floor. This framework also acknowledges the vast spectrum of positive externalities—the often-unaccounted for acts of service and care that enrich our collective well-being.
