AGI has the potential to dramatically speed up progress in most fields of human endeavor, from scientific research to technological innovation, promising to compress centuries of progress into years. This acceleration is not merely about doing things faster; it’s about fundamentally transform-ing how we approach complex problems, open-ing up new avenues of exploration, and pushing the boundaries of what’s possible. From unravel-ing the mysteries of the human body to reshaping the global economy. In the following sections, we’ll explore how AGI could revolutionize vari-ous industries and disciplines, starting with one of the most impactful areas: human health.

DRUG DISCOVERY: today, the process of identifying new pharmaceutical compounds is a time-consuming and expensive endeavor, often taking years or even decades to bring a new drug to market. With the aid of AGI, however, this process could be accelerated dramatically. By analyzing vast amounts of biological and chemical data, simulating interactions, using robots to opportunistically conduct new experiments, AGI systems could identify promising drug candidates far more quickly and efficiently than human researchers, potentially saving countless lives, improving general health, and reducing the cost of healthcare worldwide.

DeepMind’s AlphaFold system for protein structure prediction offers a glimpse of this potential. AlphaFold is a deep learning system that predicts the 3D structure of proteins based on their amino acid sequences. This task, known as protein folding, is cru-cial in understanding biological processes and has implications for drug discovery and disease research. AlphaFold leverages a transformer-based neural network architecture and is trained on large protein sequence and structure datasets. It employs an at-tention mechanism to weigh the importance of different parts of the amino acid sequence when predicting the final 3D structure.

The impact of AlphaFold lies in its accuracy and speed. It has consistently outperformed other methods in predicting protein structures, achieving results comparable to experimental techniques but at a much faster pace. AlphaFold, released in 2021, has already had a significant impact. By 2022, it had predicted structures for nearly all cataloged proteins known to science—over 200 million proteins. This breakthrough is accelerating research across biology, from developing enzymes that can break down plastic pollution to understanding complex diseases like Alzheimer’s.

In 2022, Insilico Medicine used its AI platform to design a novel drug candidate for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis. The entire pro-cess, from target discovery to preclinical candidate nomination, took only 18 months—a fraction of the time typically required.

Case study: BenevolentAI, in collaboration with AstraZeneca, used AI to identify a potential treatment for chronic kidney disease. The AI system analyzed the scientific literature and clinical data to propose baricitinib as a candidate. This drug is now in Phase IIb clinical trials, demonstrating the potential of AI to repurpose existing drugs for new treatments.

In healthcare more broadly, AGI could revolutionize the way we diagnose and treat disease. By cross-referencing medical data, in-cluding genetic information, medical records, and real-time sen-sor data, AGI systems could identify patterns and relationships that human doctors might miss, enabling earlier and more accurate di-agnoses of complex diseases like cancer and Alzheimer’s. AGI-powered robotic surgery systems could also enable more precise and less invasive surgical procedures, reducing the risk of complications and improving patient outcomes.
