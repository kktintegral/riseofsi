A notable example in the pursuit of vitality is Bryan Johnson, an entrepreneur who developed “Project Blueprint”—an intensive regimen aimed at biologi-cally reversing the aging process and maximizing human vitality. Johnson’s approach involves a strict daily routine that includes consuming a precisely calculated diet of about 1,977 calories per day, taking over 100 supplements daily, undergoing regular medical tests and interventions (including experimental treatments), adhering to a rigorous exercise and sleep schedule, and employing various devices to monitor and optimize bodily functions. Johnson is working with a team of doctors and specialists to fine-tune his vitality-enhancing efforts.

What makes Johnson’s approach interesting is that he uses himself as the primary subject of his experiments. Johnson’s self-experimentation raises intriguing questions about the boundaries between personal health optimization and broader scientific inquiry. Is this a form of pioneering research that could potentially benefit humanity at large?
