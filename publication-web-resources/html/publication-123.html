Arthur C. Clarke’s novel “Childhood’s End” offers a provocative exploration of human transformation, albeit through alien interven-tion rather than AI. The story begins with the arrival of an alien spe-cies, the Overlords, who usher in an era of peace and prosperity for humanity. However, their true purpose is revealed to be shepherd-ing humanity towards its next evolutionary step. The children of the final human generation begin to exhibit powerful psychic abilities and lose their individual identities, merging into a collective con-sciousness. This transformed humanity eventually leaves Earth to join a cosmic intelligence. While not directly about AI-driven trans-humanism, “Childhood’s End” grapples with similar themes of radi-cal human enhancement and the potential loss of traditional human identity in the face of overwhelming change. It raises profound questions about the nature of humanity and whether transcen-dence of our current form is a type of evolution or extinction.

The Transhumanist Divergence This future explores the potential for AGI and advanced biotechnology to fundamentally alter the human species. Genetic engineering and AI-designed biological enhancements lead to a diversification of human forms and capabilities. Some humans opt for radical enhancements, becoming transhuman or posthuman entities. Society grapples with questions of equality and identity as the definition of “human” becomes increasingly fluid. Multiple divergent species of humans and human-derived entities coexist, each exploring different aspects of potential.

The Cosmic Gardeners In this future, humanity and AGI to-gether take on the role of nurturing life throughout the universe. Advanced space travel and terraforming technologies allow for the seeding of life on distant planets. AGI systems help design and adapt ecosystems for alien environments. Humans, augmented by AI, serve as caretakers and guides for emerging intelligences across the galaxy. The preservation and proliferation of diverse forms of life become a central mission for human-AI civilization.

While not exactly mirroring this scenario, the concept of “Cosmic Gardeners” shares some thematic elements with the role of the Precursors in various science fiction works. In the video game series “Mass Effect,” for instance, the ancient Prothean civilization is revealed to have seeded and guided the development of many younger species across the galaxy. They left behind technology and genetic markers to influence the evolution of these species. While the Protheans were not partnered with AI in this endeavor, their role as galactic cultivators of life reflects the “Cosmic Garden-ers” concept. The game series explores the long-term conse-quences of such intervention, including the ethical implications of guiding the development of other species and the potential for unintended consequences despite benevolent intentions.
