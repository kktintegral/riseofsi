EU The European Union focuses on developing human- centric AI, prioritizing ethical considerations and responsi-ble innovation. The EU’s approach involves stringent regulations to ensure AI systems are transparent, accountable, and aligned with fundamental rights. By investing in AI through programs like Horizon Europe, the EU aims to create a robust AI ecosystem that promotes sustainable and inclusive growth.

KRSouth Korea’s AI strategy emphasizes developing domestic AI talent and promoting AI-driven economic growth. South Korea has launched initiatives to integrate AI across industries, from manufacturing to healthcare, while also investing in AI educa-tion and research. The country aims to position itself as a global AI powerhouse through its “Artificial Intelligence National Strategy.”

JP Japan invests in AI to revitalize its manufacturing sector and enhance its technological competitiveness. Japan’s Society 5.0 initiative envisions an AI-driven society where technological advancements improve quality of life. By integrating AI into its industrial base and addressing societal challenges such as an aging population, Japan aims to maintain its global economic standing.

USThe United States aims to maintain its technological edge and address national security concerns through AI re-search and development. The CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 al-located significant funding for semiconductor manufacturing, scientific research, and AI development, reflecting bipartisan recognition of AI’s strategic importance. This act is part of a broader strategy to secure supply chains, boost domestic manu-facturing, and ensure the U.S. remains at the forefront of techno-logical innovation. The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) continues to invest heavily in AI to develop next-generation defense capabilities.

HISTORICAL PARALLELS The de-velopment of AGI mirrors historical arms races, such as the nuclear arms race during the Cold War. The com-petition between the United States and the Soviet Union to develop more powerful nuclear weapons led to a dangerous period of brink-manship and the constant threat of mutually assured destruction. Simi-larly, the race to achieve AGI domi-nance could have devastating con-sequences, with nations potentially deploying AGI in ways that could spiral out of control.

The Space Race of the 1950s and 60s, while inspiring tremendous technological and scientific advanc-es, was driven by geopolitical rival-ries and the fear of falling behind in a perceived “race to the moon.” The intense competition spurred rapid innovation, but also heightened ten-sions and resource allocation driven by strategic rather than purely scientific goals.

The Manhattan Project, while successful in its immediate goal of developing the atomic bomb, gave rise to a new era of global insecurity and the looming threat of nuclear annihilation. The secrecy and urgen-cy surrounding the project are paralleled in today’s AGI research, where breakthroughs could signifi-cantly alter global power dynamics.
