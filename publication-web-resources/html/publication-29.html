AGI poses immense risks of destabilization both militarily and geopolitically. Nations that attain AGI supremacy will wield unparalleled strategic advantages in cyberwarfare, intelligence, weapons systems, and military force projection. This concentration of extreme capabilities could lead to increased temptation for unilateral aggression or preemptive strikes, further escalating global tensions.

An AI arms race between nations could accelerate the development of autonomous weapons systems (AWS), sophisticated surveillance technologies, and advanced cy-berwarfare capabilities. These au-tonomous weapons, also known as “killer robots,” are capable of se-lecting and engaging targets with-out human intervention. The de-ployment of AWS raises serious ethical and legal concerns, poten-tially resulting in a loss of human control over the use of force and increasing the risk of accidental or intentional conflict escalation. This competition could destabilize the global order, heightening the risk of conflict with catastrophic consequences.

Drones have already transformed modern warfare, as seen in the Ukraine conflict, where they play a crucial role. With AGI, these systems could become even more autonomous and efficient, further shifting the balance of power on the battlefield and amplifying the potential for both state and non-state actors to use them in conflict.

The diffuse proliferation of AGI to terrorists or non-state actors presents existential risks. These actors could exploit AGI to develop sophisticated weapons or launch cyberattacks. To counter these threats, AGI could also be employed as a policing

or counter-terrorism tool. The key challenge lies in ensuring that the cumulative capability of beneficial AGIs outweighs the threats posed by malign ones.

AGI’s superiority in persuasion and rhetoric could enable more effective and scalable influence or manipulation on a global scale. AI could be harnessed to spread misinformation and propaganda en masse. For instance, AI-powered deepfakes could manipulate public opinion, undermine democratic processes, and sow discord and division within societies, posing a significant threat to political stability.

In addition, AGI could be used to develop advanced surveillance technologies that track individuals’ movements, monitor online activities, and predict behaviors. While these capabilities might offer governments powerful tools for maintaining control, they also risk eroding privacy and civil liberties, potentially leading to the creation of surveillance states where individuals are constantly monitored and controlled.

AGI may also become indispensable for managing ultra- complex global-scale coordination problems. However, this power to govern through AGI-driven systems introduces the possibility of centralized control or manipulation on an unprecedented scale, further raising concerns about the balance between security and freedom.
