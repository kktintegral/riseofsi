The Cu-ban Missile Crisis of 1962, where the two superpowers came perilously close to nuclear war, serves as a chilling reminder of the catastrophic consequences that can arise from unchecked technological advancements and a lack of international cooperation. The crisis highlighted the importance of diplomacy and communi-cation in preventing nuclear war and underscored the need for arms control and international agreements, such as the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty.

In each of these historical instances, the pursuit of technological superiority led to both profound advancements and significant risks. The lessons learned highlight the need for careful consider-ation of ethical implications, international cooperation, and the es-tablishment of norms to prevent catastrophic outcomes.

An AI arms race could similarly escalate international tensions and mistrust, increasing the likelihood of conflict. The potential for AGI to be weaponized—whether through cyberwarfare or auton-omous weapons systems—poses a direct threat to global security. To prevent this, it is essential to establish a coalition of willing na-tions committed to developing and enforcing norms for AGI gov-ernance. This coalition should focus on creating ethical guidelines, promoting transparency, and encouraging collaboration in AGI development, while maintaining an open-door policy for other nations to join.

Rather than waiting for a full global consensus, this group could lead by example, setting standards for responsible AGI use and establishing mechanisms for accountability. Such an approach would allow for swift progress while encouraging broader participation over time. The goal would be to create a framework that discourages malicious use of AGI and mitigates the risks of conflict, while ensuring that the benefits of AGI are shared responsibly across borders.

By uniting like-minded nations under a common framework, this initiative could serve as a stabilizing force in AGI development on a global scale. It would establish a platform for dialogue, cooperation, and coordinated crisis management, helping to prevent the escalation of tensions and risks associated with unchecked technological competition. While past arms races—such as the nuclear and space races—did not result in full-scale catastrophe, they brought the world perilously close to disaster. The lessons from these near misses underscore the need for proactive engagement, shared norms, and international cooperation to ensure AGI development does not spiral out of control.
